 - War - French presidential election: A first-round runoff results between Valéry Giscard d'Estaing and François Mitterrand.
 + Science - Daniel K. Ludwig abandons the Jari project in the Amazon Basin.

 + Crime - Peter Sutcliffe is found guilty of being the Yorkshire Ripper. He is sentenced to life imprisonment on 13 counts of murder and 7 of attempted murder.
 + War - In Riyadh, the Gulf Cooperation Council is created between Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates.

 - Economy - Organization of Eastern Caribbean States founded.
 + Crime - Wayne Williams, a 23-year-old African American, is arrested and charged with the murders of 2 other African Americans. He is later accused of 28 others, in the Atlanta child murders.
 + Politics - Iranian president Abolhassan Banisadr is deposed.
 - Economy - Couples For Christ, a Christian charismatic organization, is established in the Philippines.

 + Science - California Governor Jerry Brown, faced with a Mediterranean fruit fly infestation, chooses to delay the aerial spraying of malathion, in favor of continuing ground-based eradication efforts.
